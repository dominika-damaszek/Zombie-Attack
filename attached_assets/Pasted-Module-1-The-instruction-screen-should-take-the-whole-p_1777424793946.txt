Module 2: Zombies
The zombies have now infiltrated everywhere, and they are trying to infect you. Infected traded items will turn you into a zombie in no time.
Will you survive?
Instruction 1:
The trading rules are the same as in the last module.
First, distribute 4 cards to each player.
Instruction 2:
Scan your cards.
(Cards must appear in inventory)
Instruction 3:
But now there is a difference:
There are zombies among you, and their objective is to infect survivors with contaminated items.
Survivors must trade to acquire their objective cards.
Zombies must trade to infect survivors.
Instruction 4:
You will know that you are infected by scanning your traded card at the end of a round.
If you are infected, you will start the next round as a zombie.
Instruction 5:
Your role is:
(Role appears)
Your role is an ABSOLUTE secret. Do not show or tell anyone who you are!
Instruction 6:
If you are unsure about trading with someone and there is no other option, you may skip the round.
Zombies can trade among themselves if necessary, but no points are counted for this.
ATTENTION: You can skip only one trade per game!
The round starts like the last module. The dashboard looks the same, but now includes:


Roles


The names of other zombies (visible only to zombies)


After the round, when a player scans a card:
If the scanned card is infected, a pop-up must appear explaining that:

Module 3: Passwords
Intro:
In this destroyed world, with zombies everywhere trying to get you, something curious was discovered…
Zombie brains are deteriorating, and they have great difficulty remembering information after a few hours…
That’s it!
The survivors now know how to spot the zombies:
Passwords.
Instruction 1:
Trading works like in the last modules:
Shuffle the cards and distribute 4 cards to each player.
(Scan your cards)
(Show inventory)
Instruction 2:
Roles are still secret.
Survivors must trade to complete their objectives.
Zombies must trade to infect survivors.
Instruction 3:
Passwords are the new element now!
Along with their role, survivors will now receive a password.
Example: Cat
Instruction 4:
Passwords must NEVER be spoken out loud!
Zombies are everywhere… and they can hear you!
A revealed password cannot protect anyone!
Survivors must identify each other using hints about the password.
Instruction 5:
Example: Cat
You may say:
“Do you have some clothes? I really love fluffy things.”
You may NEVER say:
“Can I have some food for my CAT?”
Example: Keyboard
You may say:
“Do you have medicine? I can write the name for you.”
You may NEVER say:
“Do you have tools? I must fix my KEYBOARD.”
Instruction 6:
Zombies will try to make you believe they know the password.
You can choose how obvious or subtle your hints are, but remember:
The more obvious you are, the easier it is for zombies to discover your password.
Remember:
Although the password can help you, your biggest defense is making the right choice.
If something feels off, it probably is.
Now your role appears together with your password, and the game continues like the last module.
Each round, a new password is assigned.
Before the end, this message will appear:
(Make the message appear in a dynamic, interesting way to capture teenagers’ attention.)
Did you trade even when you weren’t sure?
Did you get infected because you trusted without proof?
That’s what zombies… also known as social engineers… do.
They make you trust them.
They make you believe them.
They hack people.
One small mistake.
One bad decision.
One unsafe trade… and you’re done.
Your best defense is awareness.
Trust your instincts… and remember:
If something feels off, it probably is.
